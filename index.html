About

Recruiting built on systems, not spreadsheets.

I'm a full-cycle technical recruiter based in Eagle Mountain, Utah. I've spent the past four years running end-to-end searches across engineering, executive, product, sales, and operations roles — from intake call through signed offer — at high-growth startups and early-stage companies.

My background in UX design and operational systems thinking means I approach every search the way a product manager approaches a release: define the bar, build the infrastructure, measure what matters, and iterate. I don't just fill reqs — I build the systems that make hiring repeatable, scalable, and defensible.

I work entirely remotely and operate with the async discipline and proactive communication that distributed teams depend on. I manage multiple concurrent searches without needing hand-holding, and I move searches forward through organized follow-through, not recurring status meetings.

What I ran

Helped 50+ people land new roles across recruiting, interview coaching, and direct hiring support. This includes three full-cycle sole-recruiter searches: an Engineer at Inventive, an executive placement at Zapier, and Head of People at a Series A AI startup in San Francisco — all intake through signed offer.

Built recruiting infrastructure from scratch before running a single search: stage-based pipeline tracking, SLA rules, candidate communication cadences, and structured intake frameworks. Zero candidate ghosting across every search.

How I did it

Every search started with a structured intake call to define the bar before sourcing began — eliminating mid-search pivots and reducing time-to-fill. Sourcing was multi-channel: LinkedIn, GitHub for technical roles, and warm network activation. Outreach sequences were iterated based on reply rate data.

Hiring managers received pipeline dashboards showing stage conversion and search velocity without needing status calls. Candidates received same-day communication at every stage transition.

How I work

Full lifecycle, every time.

Every search I run follows the same disciplined process — from intake call to onboarding handoff.

01
Structured intake
Calibrate the bar with the hiring manager before sourcing begins. Define must-haves, red flags, and what 5/5 looks like in each competency. Write a bar doc. Get it red-penned until it's right.
02
Non-obvious sourcing
LinkedIn is table stakes. For technical roles, I go to GitHub, OSS contributions, conference talks, and community forums. The best infrastructure engineers aren't job hunting — they're committing to repos.
03
Evidence-based screening
Structured screens tied to the rubric. Every advance decision is backed by specific evidence — not vibes. Interviewers are calibrated before the first candidate arrives so scoring means something.
04
Close and onboard
Offer process starts before the panel debrief is done. Close brief prepared for every finalist. Candidate's motivators mapped. No surprises. Onboarding handoff documented so their first day matches the pitch.
